Policy & vehicle identification

Policy number / license plate / VIN
Policyholder identification and contact (phone, email, preferred channel)
Who is reporting? (policyholder, driver, claimant, repair shop, lawyer, broker) — the reporter type changes the entire downstream workflow

Accident circumstances

Date and time of loss (and time of report — the delay matters for duty-of-disclosure and fraud signals)
Location: full address, country, road type (highway / rural road / urban / parking lot)
Weather, visibility, road conditions
Type of loss: moving traffic collision / stationary vehicle / parking damage / wildlife / animal / property damage without third party / personal injury
Free-form description of how the accident happened, in the reporter's own words
Direction of travel for both parties, estimated speed, right-of-way rules (traffic light, sign, right-before-left)
Sketch or photos of the scene available?
European Accident Statement filled out and signed by both parties?

Driver (critical for coverage)

Was the policyholder driving? If not: name, date of birth, relationship to policyholder
Valid driver's license, class, current?
Included in the policy's driver scope?
Alcohol / drugs / medication involved? Breathalyzer test conducted?
Health limitations relevant to the incident?
Hit-and-run? (immediately triggers a duty-of-disclosure breach review)

Other party / claimant

Name, address, phone, email
License plate, vehicle (make/model)
Insurer and policy number of the other party (for recourse or direct settlement)
Registered owner ≠ driver? Capture both records
Did the other party admit or deny fault?
Have they already raised claims (letter, lawyer, their own insurer)?

Police & authorities

Police on scene? Station, case number, reference number
Fines or criminal proceedings indicated?
Administrative offense, criminal offense (e.g. hit-and-run, dangerous driving, DUI)?

Witnesses

Independent witnesses (name, contact, brief statement)
Passengers on either side

Personal injuries

Injured parties (own / passengers / other party / third parties / pedestrians / cyclists)
Type of injury, treatment (outpatient / inpatient), hospital / doctor
Health insurer of the injured (for subrogation under social insurance recovery rules)
Anticipated time off work?

Property damage

Damage to own vehicle: description, photos, visible areas
Drivable? Current location of the vehicle? Towed by whom, to where?
Damage to the other party's vehicle
Third-party property (guardrail, traffic light, fence, building → owner, possibly municipality)
Pre-existing damage in the affected area? (relevant for fraud and quota)

Liability indicators

Did anyone admit fault at the scene — and was it documented in writing? (limited evidentiary value, but relevant)
Right-of-way violation, red light, following distance, lane change, reversing — targeted questions depending on the type of incident
Prima facie evidence constellations (rear-end, reversing, lane change) explicitly checked

Duties of disclosure / coverage exclusions (policy-condition-relevant)

Use outside the contractual scope? (commercial use instead of private, paid passenger transport)
Racing or skill-based event?
Unregistered modifications?
Vehicle inspection / emissions test current?
Use outside the geographic coverage area?
Late reporting — reason?

Recourse indicators

Other party's insurer known → direct recourse
Third-party causation (repair shop, prior owner, manufacturer in case of product defect)
Municipality / road authority for road-condition damage
Social insurance carrier involvement in personal injury cases

Fraud signals (partly inferable from other answers, but worth asking explicitly)

When exactly was the damage noticed vs. reported?
Are the parties known to each other or related?
Similar claims in the last 24 months?
Is the vehicle currently listed for sale?
Strikingly high damage relative to the vehicle's residual value?

Settlement preferences

Repair shop steering? Preferred shop (limited relevance for liability, but important for steering)
Rental car / loss-of-use compensation requested?
Lawyer involved? (name, firm) — completely changes the communication channel
Preferred communication channel (email, mail, portal)
