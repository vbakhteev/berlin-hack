# 05 - Berlin & European Hackathon Winners (Agent 1.5)

Date: 2026-04-21. Pipeline phase 1. Closest jury signal for Big Berlin Hack (2nd edition), {Tech:Europe} x CODE University x The Delta Campus.

## TL;DR (5 lines)

1. European juries reward **substance over polish**: working code, a clear B2B/industrial/deep-tech wedge, and a real domain insight beat slick UI demos.
2. The {Tech:Europe} Berlin 1st edition winners (GenY, Professor Dr. Scientist, LLM Studios) were picked from 26 teams by a jury of operator/VC types (Leonard Langenscheidt, Mish Ushakov, Azer Kocu) - signal is **builder-jury, not pure VC**, meaning technical demos and live working code matter more than pitch decks.
3. Across EU hackathons in the last 12 months (Mistral Paris, ElevenLabs London/Warsaw, TUM.ai, EDTH Munich, UCL Great Agent Hack, Hello Tomorrow), the winning pattern is: **pick a specific, non-obvious vertical (defense, bereavement admin, insurance underwriting, airspace safety, dermatology), show a novel protocol or mechanism (not just "GPT wrapper"), and have a 30-second moment where something tangible happens live**.
4. "GibberLink" (ElevenLabs London) - two AI agents realizing on a phone call that they are both AI and switching to a faster audio protocol - is the single most copied stage-wow pattern of 2025: **reveal a live, unexpected capability in the first 30 seconds**.
5. European taste skews away from crypto/consumer-vibe-coding toward defense, climate, health, manufacturing, materials, and "boring B2B that saves a regulated industry 6 hours per case". Less Twitter-demo energy, more "this could actually ship Monday".

## 1. European jury patterns (what wins in Europe vs SF)

1. **B2B / vertical wedge beats horizontal consumer toys.** Insurance underwriting (FairQuote, UCL), debt collection (Claimsio, Warsaw), employee onboarding sims (London 2nd place), airspace safety (DeepSky, Warsaw), IVF hormone monitoring (Impli, Hello Tomorrow) all placed. Consumer social/meme apps almost never place in EU.
2. **Deep-tech / regulated-industry bias.** Hello Tomorrow Grand Prize 2025 went to Numi (cultured human breast milk). EDTH Munich winners all defense/drone swarms. This reflects the jury pool (Merantix, Helsing alumni, Entrepreneur First deep-tech partners, ETH/TUM academics).
3. **A novel protocol or mechanism, not a new UX wrapper.** GibberLink's ggwave audio protocol, HSIA's semantic injection on robot vision, Jailbreak Lab's genetic-algorithm prompt mutation - all won because they introduced a **mechanism** the jury hadn't seen. "Another agent with nicer chat UI" loses.
4. **Multi-agent coordination with clear role separation** (FairQuote, Delego). EU judges want to see the system diagram, not just one LLM call.
5. **Observability / evaluation / eval harnesses are rewarded.** Great Agent Hack judges explicitly named "production-ready architecture with monitoring and observability" as a differentiator. SF would call this "boring infra"; EU calls it "real engineering".
6. **Climate / energy / materials gets a bonus.** Found Energy (aluminum fuel, Hello Tomorrow), the LLM Materials Science hackathon, Berlin Energy Hackathon, Reonic being a Big Berlin Hack track partner - climate/energy is a persistent EU theme.
7. **Health + diagnostics without the hype.** Impli (IVF), DermAssist (Mistral Paris), Eyestral (vision for blind, Mistral Paris Winner), Mental Health Triage (London 3rd). Narrow, clinical, not "AI doctor".
8. **Defense tech is now mainstream and wins prizes.** EDTH Munich/Amsterdam had 250+ hackers, Sifted covers it seriously. Drone swarms, GPS-denied navigation, Shahed intercept, underwater recon are mainline prize categories. Historically taboo in SF consumer hackathons; now explicitly funded in EU.
9. **Live working code trumps video demos.** AI Tinkerers rule: "every demo must show running code, no slides allowed". Berlin and Zurich inherit this norm. Demo-factor judges discount pre-recorded screen captures heavily.
10. **Genuine human-impact framing wins the room.** AfterVerse (bereavement admin agent, UCL 1st) won largely on "real human impact" per judges. EU juries will reward tear-jerker use cases if the tech is also solid. This is a BIG delta from SF.
11. **Research-to-practice bridges are prized.** Arxflix (papers-to-video, Mistral Paris Winner), GenAI Explainer (FLUX interpretability), Uncooked (Socratic AI tutor). EU academic-industrial pipeline rewards "I turned a paper into a tool".
12. **"Unfair advantage" tools: multi-modal + sensors + robotics.** Le ChatOn Vision (robot video analysis), BCI controls (AgentsIRL Paris), EDTH drone-swarm search. Using hardware or sensor input that most SF teams can't reach in 36 hours gets extra points.
13. **Weaviate / Mistral / ElevenLabs / Lovable sponsor credits shape the tech stack.** Judges from these companies reward non-trivial use of their stack. Using Mistral for a genuinely European/sovereign-AI angle is a known +1. Being "just OpenAI" is neutral; showing RAG-over-Weaviate-plus-Mistral-with-ElevenLabs-voice is the house style.
14. **Understated delivery, no "I've been working on this for 3 years" theatrics.** Sifted coverage and HackZurich reputation skew toward calm, technical presentation. Overly theatrical SF-style pitches read as cringe.
15. **Trust / safety / red-teaming is a track, not an afterthought.** "Dear Grandma" entire track at UCL, Holistic AI partner, "responsible AI" at VivaTech. Adding a safety/eval layer to an otherwise-fun hack can move you from mid to top 3.

## 2. {Tech:Europe} / Big Berlin Hack specific signal

Source: https://blog.techeurope.io/p/ai-hackathon-1-wrap-up and https://luma.com/bigberlinhack

- **1st edition jury (Aug 2024, Factory Network Berlin)**: Leonard Langenscheidt, Mish Ushakov (ex-Browser-Use/Trigger.dev type builder crowd), Azer Kocu, Ege Gunduz, Kasia Stoltmann. This is a **builder-operator jury**, not a VC-partner jury. Mish Ushakov in particular reads as deep automation / AI-agents infra.
- **26 teams submitted in 1st edition. Top 3: GenY, Professor Dr. Scientist, LLM Studios.** Winners received credits for Mistral, Weaviate, Luma AI plus Factory membership. Specific project one-liners are NOT public - this is a deliberate signal that team branding + demo > detailed write-up.
- **Infra partners for 2nd edition (Big Berlin Hack, this weekend)**: Google DeepMind, Lovable, Gradium, Entire, Tavily, Pioneer, Aikido, Weaviate, Mistral still in the ecosystem.
- **Track partners**: Buena (proptech), Qontext, Inca, Hera, Peec AI, Reonic (climate/solar SaaS), telli (voice AI for calls), ai-coustics (audio processing). **These are all B2B SaaS / vertical AI companies**, confirming the vertical-wedge bias.
- **Organizer triangle signal**: {Tech:Europe} = builder community, CODE University = applied CS / product-engineering students, The Delta Campus = deep-tech/compute infra. Combination skews toward **"deep-tech-feasible product you could spin out"**, not pure-research and not pure-consumer.
- **Prize structure**: $50k total across tracks. Track-partner prizes suggest **matching your hack to a sponsor's problem domain** (proptech -> Buena, voice -> telli, solar -> Reonic, audio -> ai-coustics) is a smart bet. Winning a single vertical track may be easier than the grand prize.
- **Format**: 36h hack, finalist cut at 3pm Sunday, 3:15pm pitches, 4:30pm awards. Means **pitches are ~5 min with live demo** and the jury has <90 min to pick. Optimize for a visible-in-60-seconds moment.
- **Vibe**: "Build something you always wanted to do, but never did." Not "solve this industry's RFP". This leaves room for creative personal-itch projects but the track-partner structure pulls you back toward B2B utility.
